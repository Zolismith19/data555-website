About Me

I am an MPH candidate in Epidemiology at the Rollins School of Public Health at Emory University, and an incoming PhD student in Health Science Informatics at Johns Hopkins Medicine. My work sits at the intersection of epidemiology, data science, and health equity, with a particular focus on transgender health, cardiovascular outcomes, and reproducible research methods.

My current MPH thesis examines time-to-event analyses of cardiovascular outcomes including venous thromboembolism, ischemic stroke, and myocardial infarction among transmasculine individuals receiving testosterone therapy, using the STRONG cohort from Kaiser Permanente (N approximately 345,000). In parallel, I work as a research assistant in Dr. Chang Su’s single-cell genomics lab, investigating enhancer-gene interactions in Alzheimer’s disease using multi-omic data.

I am passionate about making complex data accessible through interactive dashboards for clinicians, reproducible pipelines for collaborators, and accessible visualizations for policy audiences. I graduated summa cum laude from Spelman College in 2024, where I was a top 5 graduate and Ethell Wadell Githii Honors Scholar.

Research Interests

Cardiovascular Epidemiology

Studying CVD risk disparities in transgender populations using large EHR-based cohort data and survival models.

Genomics and Informatics

Analyzing single-cell multi-omic data to investigate transcriptional regulation and neurodegeneration in Alzheimer’s disease.

Data Science and Visualization

Building reproducible pipelines and interactive dashboards in R to communicate research findings to diverse audiences.

Health Equity

Investigating disparities in cancer staging, transgender health outcomes, and global nutrition policy.
